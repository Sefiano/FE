# Phase One: Bazaar Digital Flow

Phase One should now be framed as:

A complete digital operating layer for Shababco's bazaar: consumer ticketing, vendor booking, booth/package control, event product visibility, POS sales capture, and admin reporting.

---

## Section 1 — Strategic Output of Phase One

### Card 1: Better Event Experience
Consumers get a cleaner event journey.
They can discover the event, understand the experience, view participating brands, and buy tickets directly through Shababco's own ticketing system.

### Card 2: Better Vendor Experience
Brands get a more professional booking journey.
They can book booths, receive confirmation, connect their store or event profile, and prepare their bazaar catalog more clearly.

### Card 3: Better Internal Control
Shababco gets one central dashboard for events, tickets, vendors, booths, booking status, product visibility, and event sales data.
This reduces operational friction and gives the team a clearer view of each bazaar.

### Card 4: Better Marketplace Foundation
The most important output is not only a better bazaar experience.
The most important output is that Shababco starts building a structured vendor, consumer, ticketing, product, and transaction database that can later support the year-round marketplace.

---

## Section 2 — Consumer Experience

### Card 1: Event Landing Page
Consumers land on a clean Shababco event page.
They see the event identity, date, venue, visuals, schedule, artists, participating brands, and key experience highlights.

### Card 2: Discovery Before the Event
The page helps consumers understand what they are coming for before they buy a ticket.
This can include featured brands, event collections, product previews, sponsor sections, and the overall event story.

### Card 3: Native Ticket Purchase
Consumers buy tickets directly through Shababco's own ticketing system.
The flow should support ticket types, pricing, availability, confirmation, and ticket IDs/QR codes.

### Card 4: Post-Event Continuity
After the event, the same page can become a post-event destination.
Consumers can revisit brands, see highlights, browse products, and continue into the future marketplace experience.

---

## Section 3 — Vendor Booking Flow

### Card 1: Vendor Entry Point
Brands enter through the Shababco website.
Instead of informal communication or scattered forms, the website becomes the official vendor booking gateway.

### Card 2: Booth Package Selection
Brands choose from available booth packages.
Each package can have its own price, size, design, benefits, location tier, or visibility level.

### Card 3: Booking Confirmation
Once a brand books, the system creates a unique vendor/event ID.
This ID connects the brand to the event and becomes the bridge into product selection, POS usage, and future marketplace onboarding.

### Card 4: Cleaner Vendor Operations
Shababco can track who booked, who paid, who is pending, which package they selected, and which brands are participating in each event.
This turns vendor booking into structured operational data.

---

## Section 4 — Admin Dashboard

### Card 1: Event Control
The admin team can create and update event pages without depending on developers for every change.
They can update images, descriptions, dates, venue information, schedules, sponsor sections, and event catalogs.

### Card 2: Ticketing Control
The admin team can create ticket types, adjust pricing, set availability, view buyers, track issued tickets, and monitor event attendance data.
This gives Shababco ownership over the ticketing layer instead of depending fully on external routing.

### Card 3: Booth and Vendor Control
The admin team can create booth packages, adjust availability, manage pricing, view bookings, confirm payments, approve vendors, and decide which brands appear publicly.

### Card 4: Product and Sales Visibility
Once brands connect product data and use the event POS, the admin team can see which SKUs are being brought, what is selling, and how the event is performing commercially.

---

## Section 5 — POS and Event Data Loop

### Card 1: Brand Verification
After booking, a Shopify brand installs the app or enters through the event POS flow using its vendor/event ID.
The system verifies that the brand is actually participating in the event.

### Card 2: Bazaar SKU Selection
The brand selects which products or SKUs it plans to bring to the bazaar.
This gives Shababco visibility into the event's product mix before the event starts.

### Card 3: Event POS / Sales Capture
During the bazaar, the brand uses a lightweight POS or sales logger for event-specific transactions.
The goal is to capture SKU-level sales movement, not overload the brand with a complex retail system.

### Card 4: Admin Data Loop
Sales, SKU movement, vendor performance, category performance, and price-point signals flow back into the admin dashboard.
This completes the loop:

```text
Vendor Booking → SKU Selection → Bazaar Sales → Admin Insights → Marketplace Readiness
```
